# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud 

## What is UPI Fraud? 
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of online financial crime in which fraudsters 
trick victims into approving fraudulent payment requests or revealing sensitive credentials such 
as UPI PINs and OTPs. Unlike traditional banking fraud, UPI fraud exploits the speed and ease 
of digital payments, making it particularly dangerous for first-time users and college students.

## How It Typically Happens 
1. A fraudster contacts the victim via phone call, SMS, or WhatsApp, posing as a bank 
representative, customer support executive, or a buyer on an online marketplace. 
2. The fraudster creates a sense of urgency — for example, claiming the victim's account 
will be blocked or that they need to verify their KYC immediately. 
3. The victim is asked to share a one-time password (OTP), scan a QR code, or click a 
collect request on their UPI app — which the fraudster claims is a refund or verification 
step. 
4. The moment the victim approves the request or shares the OTP, money is instantly 
debited from their account. 
5. The fraudster disappears and the transaction, being digital and instant, is nearly 
impossible to reverse.

## Who is Usually Targeted? 
First-time UPI users, college students, senior citizens, and individuals selling products on 
platforms such as OLX or Facebook Marketplace are the most common targets. Students are 
particularly vulnerable because they frequently use UPI for peer-to-peer payments, hostel fees, 
and online shopping, often without fully understanding how collect requests work. 

## Consequences 
Victims suffer immediate financial loss, which is rarely recovered due to the irreversible nature 
of UPI transactions. Beyond financial damage, victims often experience significant emotional 
distress, anxiety, and a loss of trust in digital payment systems. In some cases, repeated targeting 
can lead to identity theft if the fraudster also collects personal documents during the interaction.
